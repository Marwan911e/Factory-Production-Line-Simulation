Queueing Theory Metrics

This section presents metrics calculated using queueing theory principles.

Average Waiting Time = Total time Customer wait in queue / Total number of Customers

Probability (Wait) = number of customer who wait(count) / Total number of customer

Queue length = sum of all in queue / total number of customer

Probability of idle = Total idle time of Server / Total run time in Simulation

Average Service Time = Total Service Time / Total number of customer

Average time between arrivals = sum of interarrival times / total number of Customers -1

Average waiting time for those who wait = Total time Customer wait in queue / Total number of Customers who wait(count)

Server Utilization = 1 - probability of idle

Understanding Utilization and Waiting Time:

Even with moderate utilization rates (e.g., 67%), long waiting times can occur. This is because as utilization increases, waiting times grow exponentially, not linearly:

  • At 50% utilization, relative waiting time = 1x
  • At 67% utilization, relative waiting time = 2x
  • At 80% utilization, relative waiting time = 4x
  • At 90% utilization, relative waiting time = 9x
  • At 95% utilization, relative waiting time = 19x

This explains why bottlenecks form even when machines aren't running at 100% capacity.
